About the program:

The project concentrates on Major Haemoglobin Disorders (MHD) which is a significant public health issue in the country. In Odisha about 10% of the population are estimated to be carriers or with disease.

Transfusion and Chelation are only supportive therapies with risk of major organ disfunction seen in nearly half of the patients. Bone Marrow Transplant (BMT) though found curative are not widely available, especially in the public health sector and depending on the private health care sector may lead to catastrophic health expenditure.

The project aims to bring down the burden of these diseases in the affected populations in Odisha with the combined effort of Ministry of Health- Odisha, NHM Odisha, Christian Medical College, Vellore and Centre for Stem Cell Research, a unit of inStem, Bengaluru with the support of Department of Biotechnology of the Ministry of Science and Technology, Government of India.

Priorities of project:

1. To increase awareness on MHD, its genetic transmission and options for preventing it

2. To increase access to testing, counselling and pre-natal diagnosis

3. To counsel and screen individuals and couples; give relevant information and connect them to appropriate additional services;

4. Provide training to healthcare workers for improved diagnosis and treatment of MHD

5. Support partnerships among different government agencies, non-governmental organizations and civil society for this program.

Priority groups:

1. Pregnant Women & Spouse

2. Adolescents and Young Adults

Globally, there is no precedence of a project of this magnitude that aims to systematically cover a population of about 45 million in a defined span of time with communication, counselling, testing and follow up services. One of the novel approaches in this project is also to introduce a two-step test laboratory evaluation of carrier status combining screening and confirmation without the need of repeating blood sample collection. Both screening and confirmatory tests can be done using a single sample.
